Key parameters

The four numbers that run the recipe.

French press is forgiving — but getting these four dials right means the difference between a cup that's muddy and bitter and one that's clean and bold.

Dose
30g
For a standard 450g brew (about 2 cups). Scale up by the same ratio — 60g for 900g water fills most 8-cup presses.
Ratio 1:15
450g water
One gram of coffee per 15 grams of water. Slightly stronger than pour-over since the metal filter passes more body into the cup.
Grind
Coarse
Like rough sea salt or cracked peppercorns. Too fine and the mesh won't filter it, leaving the cup muddy and bitter.
Water temp
93–96°C
Just off the boil — about 30 seconds after the kettle clicks off. French press can handle higher temps than pour-over because the steep time compensates.
Brew guide · 4 minutes total

A timeline, not a technique.

French press is the most time-based brew method. The steps are simple — the clock is the only thing to watch.

0:00 — Start

Heat water and preheat the press

Bring water to 94°C (or let boiling water sit 30 seconds). Pour a small amount into the press to warm the glass, swirl, and discard. A cold press drops water temperature by 5–8°C immediately — preheating keeps the steep at the right temp throughout.

0:00 — Add coffee

Grind coarse and dose

Grind 30g of coffee to the coarseness of rough sea salt. Add it to the warmed press and shake gently to level the bed. Place the press on your scale and zero it.

Grind right before brewing. Coarse grounds go stale faster than fine ones once exposed to air.
0:00 → 0:30 — Bloom

Pour 60g of water and wait

Start your timer. Pour just enough hot water to saturate the grounds — about double the coffee weight (60g). Stir gently with a spoon to make sure all the grounds are wet. Wait 30 seconds while the coffee blooms and off-gases CO₂.

0:30 — Fill

Pour the remaining water

Pour the rest of your water (to 450g total) in a slow, even spiral. Stir once more to ensure full saturation. Place the lid on the press with the plunger pulled all the way up — do not press yet.

0:30 → 4:00 — Steep

Wait 3.5 minutes without disturbing

Set a timer. Don't swirl, stir, or lift the lid. The grounds need an undisturbed immersion for full, even extraction. Use this time to warm your mugs with hot water.

Steep time is your strength dial. 3.5–4 min = balanced. 5+ min = very bold and potentially bitter.
4:00 — Plunge

Press slowly and steadily

When the timer hits 4:00, press the plunger down with firm, even pressure. It should take about 20–30 seconds to reach the bottom. If it shoots down instantly, the grind is too coarse. If it's impossible to push, too fine.

4:30 — Pour immediately

Don't let it sit in the press

Pour every drop into cups or a separate carafe right after plunging. Leaving brewed coffee sitting on the grounds — even with the plunger down — causes over-extraction and bitterness. The press is not a carafe.

Pour it all out. If you're not drinking it immediately, transfer to a thermos.
Tips

Fix the muddy cup. Keep the bold one.

Grind coarse — really coarse

The most common French press mistake is grinding too fine. The metal mesh doesn't trap fines — they pass through and make the cup gritty and bitter. When in doubt, go one step coarser than you think you need.

Skim the crust before plunging

After the steep, a crust of grounds floats on top. Use a spoon to break it and skim off the foam and loose grounds before placing the lid. This removes some of the bitterness that concentrates at the surface.

Don't plunge too fast

Plunging quickly spikes pressure and forces fines through the mesh into the cup. Press slow and steady — 20 to 30 seconds, even pressure. If you feel significant resistance, stop rather than force it.

Pour out everything

The moment the plunge is done, pour the entire brew into cups or a carafe. Coffee left in contact with the grounds continues extracting and will be unpleasantly bitter within 5 minutes.

Clean it properly

Rinse the press immediately after use — wet grounds are easy to rinse out. Dried grounds require disassembly and scrubbing. The mesh plunger should be taken apart and rinsed separately weekly to prevent rancid oil buildup.

Dial in with time, not grind

Because coarse grinding is so important for clarity, use steep time as your primary strength adjustment. Prefer 3.5 minutes for a cleaner cup, 4.5 minutes for maximum body. Adjust grind only if time-changes aren't giving you what you want.
