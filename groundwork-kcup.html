Get the best from your pod.

Cup size matters

The single biggest choice you make.

Every pod contains the same amount of coffee. The cup size you pick changes how much water runs through it — and that changes the strength entirely.

6 oz

Full extraction, concentrated flavour. The cup a K-Cup was designed to make.

Brew guide

Step by step.

There's not much to change — but what you can control, you should. Follow the steps, then run the 60-second timer once the pod is in.

Step 01

Use filtered water

Fill the reservoir with filtered water, or let tap water sit overnight to off-gas chlorine. Hard tap water leaves mineral buildup that clogs needles and mutes flavour. Most of what's in the cup is water — treat it accordingly.

Step 02

Pre-heat the machine and your mug

Run a water-only cycle (no pod) before you brew. This flushes old residue, brings the water to full temperature, and pre-warms the internal lines. Fill your mug with the hot water, let it sit for 30 seconds, then discard it.

Cold mugs pull 5–10°C out of your brew instantly. A pre-warmed mug keeps the cup hotter, longer.
Step 03

Choose a fresh pod

Check the best-by date on the pod. K-Cups are nitrogen-flushed and sealed, so they last 1–2 years unopened — but flavour declines noticeably after that. Store pods in a cool, dark place, not in direct sunlight or near a heat source.

Step 04

Set the smallest cup size and brew

Lift the handle, drop in the pod, and close it firmly. Select 6 oz for the strongest, most flavour-forward cup. Press brew — then start the timer below.

Pro tips

Small changes, noticeably better cup.

Descale every 3 months

Mineral scale insulates the heating element, making the water cooler and brew time longer. Run descaling solution or 1:1 white vinegar and water, then two full water rinse cycles.

Try reusable pods

A reusable K-Cup filter lets you use freshly ground coffee of your choice. Grind medium-fine, fill to just below the rim, and don't pack it tightly. The results are noticeably better than any pre-ground pod.

Strong mode, if you have it

Many Keurig models have a "Strong" button that slows the brew cycle by 30–45 seconds. This increases extraction time and produces a noticeably fuller, more flavourful cup without changing the volume.

Match roast to size

Light roasts at 6 oz can taste sharp and sour. Dark roasts hold up better to larger sizes. If you prefer 10 oz, choose a medium-dark or dark roast pod.

Keep the lid closed

Don't lift the handle while water is still dripping. Opening mid-brew drops pressure and can spray grounds. Wait for the full cycle to finish.

Clean the needle monthly

The entry and exit needles get clogged with grounds over time. Use the Keurig needle-cleaning tool or a straightened paperclip to clear the holes. A clogged needle makes the cup over-extracted and bitter.
