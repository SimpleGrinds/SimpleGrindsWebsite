Key parameters

The numbers that matter.

These are the levers. Understanding each one — and what it tastes like when it's wrong — is the whole game.

Dose
18–20g
Ground coffee into the basket. Single: 7–9g. Double (standard): 18–20g. Weigh it — don't estimate.
Yield (ratio 1:2)
36–40g
Liquid espresso out. A 1:2 ratio means 18g in, 36g out. Weigh your output for consistency.
Brew time
25–30s
From first drop to last. Start timing when you press the button, stop when you hit your target yield.
Water temp
92–94°C
Lighter roasts pull better at 93–94°C. Darker roasts at 91–92°C. Too hot = bitter; too cool = sour.
Brew guide

Step by step.

Each step is short. The margin for error is also short. Do them in order, every time, until it's muscle memory.

Step 01

Choose and grind fresh

Use beans roasted within 2–6 weeks. Grind immediately before pulling the shot — espresso goes stale in minutes once ground. Target a grind so fine it resembles powdered sugar, but not flour.

Aim: a pinch between two fingers should barely clump. If it flows freely, go finer.
Step 02

Dose the basket

Weigh 18–20g into a clean, dry basket. If you're using a single basket, 7–9g. Knock the portafilter gently on your palm to settle the grounds, then level the surface with a straight edge or finger swipe.

Step 03

Tamp evenly

Place the tamper on the grounds and apply firm, level pressure — roughly 15–20 kg, straight down. The surface must be flat and parallel to the basket edge. A tilted tamp causes channelling: water rushes through the easy path.

Check: the tamped puck surface should look uniform, with no cracks or loose edges.
Step 04

Flush and lock in

Run a 2-second flush (blank shot) through the group head to purge old water and stabilise temperature. Lock in the portafilter and place a scale with your cup underneath. Zero the scale.

Step 05

Pull the shot

Start the shot and start the timer simultaneously. Watch the flow: it should begin within 5–8 seconds and pour in a steady, thin stream — like warm honey. Stop when the scale reads your target yield (36–40g for an 18g dose).

Target: 36g out in 27 seconds for an 18g dose. This is your reference point — adjust from here.
Step 06

Taste and dial in

Taste the shot immediately. Use the table below to read what went wrong — or right — and adjust one variable at a time. Never adjust both grind and dose simultaneously.

Troubleshooting

Read the shot, fix the shot.

Espresso tells you exactly what's wrong. Tap the symptom your shot is showing.

Likely cause
Under-extracted — water moved too fast through the puck, or temperature was too low.
→ Grind finer (one step at a time). Or increase water temp by 1°C.
Likely cause
Over-extracted — shot ran too long, grind too fine, or water too hot.
→ Grind coarser. Or reduce water temp by 1°C. Stop the shot earlier.
Likely cause
Yield too high — you let the shot run too long and diluted it.
→ Stop earlier. Reduce target yield by 4g and taste.
Likely cause
Yield too low — the shot was stopped too early.
→ Increase target yield toward 40g. Let it run a few more seconds.
Likely cause
Tamped unevenly — water found the path of least resistance and shot through a crack.
→ Distribute grounds evenly before tamping. Tamp level and firm. Check basket edge.
Likely cause
Grind too fine or dose too high — puck is too dense for 9 bar to push through.
→ Go one click coarser. Remove 0.5g from your dose. Check tamping pressure.
Pro tips

The details that separate good from great.

Weigh everything

A scale under the portafilter is non-negotiable. Scoops lie. Eyeballing lies. 18g in and 36g out, every time, is the only way to get repeatable results.

Change one variable

If you adjust the grind and the dose at the same time, you won't know which one fixed it. Pick one, pull a shot, taste, then decide the next move.

Let your machine warm up

A cold group head kills the shot temperature. Run a blank flush for 20–30 seconds, then let it sit for a minute before pulling. Thermal stability matters more than any other variable.

Keep it clean

Old coffee oils go rancid and ruin fresh shots. Backflush the group head daily; wipe the basket and portafilter after every shot. A clean machine tastes better.

Rest your beans

Beans need to off-gas after roasting. Too fresh (under 5 days) and CO₂ causes uneven extraction and hollow flavour. Too old (over 6 weeks) and they're stale. 2–4 weeks post-roast is the sweet spot.

Use fresh, filtered water

Espresso is 90% water. Mineral-rich water helps extraction; softened or distilled water hurts it. Aim for water around 150 mg/L total dissolved solids.
